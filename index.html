About Me

I am Taous Meriem Laleg, a senior researcher at the National Institute for Research in Digital Science and Technology (INRIA), Paris-Saclay, France. I was an assistant professor and then associate professor at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST), Saudi Arabia. In KAUST, I lead the Estimation Modeling and Analysis (EMAN) group at KAUST. I received my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics in 2008 from INRIA in the Paris area and Versailles University. Afterward, I joined KAUST in 2011 after being a researcher at INRIA Bordeaux. My work encompasses the fields of mathematical control theory, systems modeling, signal processing, and their applications. My primary research goals are directed toward developing effective estimation methods and algorithms to understand complex systems, extract hidden information, and design control and monitoring strategies. I am greatly motivated by real-world problems in the engineering and biomedical fields. .

Research

Professor Laleg-Kirati's research interests encompass work across the fields of applied mathematics, control systems, and signal analysis. She works on new methods for signal analysis based on a semi-classical approach with an application to the analysis of the arterial blood pressure. Laleg-Kirati is also interested in modeling, identification, control, fault detection, inverse problems and especially seismic inversion and has expertise in solitons waves and scattering theory.
